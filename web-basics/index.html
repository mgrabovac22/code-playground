Plot of a movie

Kingdom of Heaven is a 2005 epic historical drama film directed and produced by Ridley Scott and written by William Monahan. In medieval France, Balian, a blacksmith who is haunted by his wife's recent suicide after the death of their unborn child, meets a group of Crusaders who visit his village. Their leader introduces himself as Balian's father, Baron Godfrey of Ibelin, and asks him to return with him to the Holy Land, but Balian declines. Later that night, Balian's half-brother, the town priest, reveals that he ordered Balian's wife's body beheaded before burial. Upon discovering that the priest also stole his wife's crucifix, Balian kills him. The next day, Balian joins his father's group, hoping to gain forgiveness and redemption for himself and his wife in Jerusalem. They are soon confronted by soldiers sent to arrest Balian and Godfrey is struck by an arrow. The group reaches Messina where they have a contentious encounter with Guy de Lusignan, a prospective future king of Jerusalem who supports the brutal anti-Muslim Templar Knights and intends to break the fragile alliance between the Crusader states and Sultan Saladin. A night before sailing to the Holy Land, Godfrey knights Balian, names him the new Baron of Ibelin.

A night before sailing to the Holy Land, Godfrey knights Balian, names him the new Baron of Ibelin, and orders him to serve the King of Jerusalem and protect the helpless before succumbing to his arrow wound. Balian sails for the Holy Land but his ship runs aground in a storm, leaving him the only survivor.

As he makes his way to Jerusalem on foot, Balian is confronted by a Muslim cavalier who attacks him for his horse. Balian slays the cavalier but spares his servant, who accompanies him to Jerusalem. Once in the city, Balian frees the servant who tells him that his mercy will gain him fame and respect among the Saracens. Balian quickly becomes acquainted with Jerusalem's political arena: the leper King Baldwin IV, Tiberias the Marshal of Jerusalem, and the King's sister Princess Sibylla, Guy's wife and mother to a boy from an earlier marriage. Balian then travels to his inherited estate at Ibelin and, using his engineering knowledge, he helps the struggling residents irrigate the land, earning him their love and respect in the process. Sibylla visits him and they become lovers.

Music in a film

The music differs in style and content from the soundtrack of Scott's earlier 2000 film Gladiator and many other subsequent films depicting historical events. A combination of medieval, Middle Eastern, contemporary classical, and popular influences, the soundtrack is largely the work of British film-score composer Harry Gregson-Williams.

Box office

The film was a box office disappointment in the US and Canada, earning $47.4 million against a budget of around $130 million, but did better in Europe and the rest of the world, earning $164.3 million, with the worldwide box office earnings totalling at $211,643,158
